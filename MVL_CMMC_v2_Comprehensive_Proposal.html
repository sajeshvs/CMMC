1.1 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Business Challenge

MVL Group is embarking on a critical journey to strengthen its cybersecurity posture and achieve compliance with the Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC) v2 and NIST SP 800-171 Rev 2 standards for the protection of Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI).

What is CMMC? The Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification is a unified cybersecurity standard mandated by the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) for all defense contractors and subcontractors in the Defense Industrial Base (DIB). It ensures that sensitive defense information is adequately protected across the entire supply chain. What is CUI? Controlled Unclassified Information refers to sensitive information that requires safeguarding or dissemination controls pursuant to federal law, regulations, and government policies. Examples include technical data, contract details, procurement information, and export-controlled data.

Why This Matters for MVL

As a defense contractor or supplier within the Defense Industrial Base (DIB), MVL Group must demonstrate robust cybersecurity controls to:

  • Protect CUI from unauthorized access, theft, and compromise - Ensure sensitive defense information remains secure throughout its lifecycle (creation, storage, transmission, and disposal)
  • Meet contractual obligations under DFARS 252.204-7012 - Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement clause requires contractors to implement NIST SP 800-171 security requirements and report cyber incidents within 72 hours
  • Qualify for Department of Defense (DoD) contracts requiring CMMC certification - Starting 2025-2026, CMMC certification will become a prerequisite for bidding on and winning DoD contracts involving CUI
  • Establish a sustainable compliance program aligned with federal cybersecurity standards - Build a long-term framework that adapts to evolving threats and regulatory requirements
